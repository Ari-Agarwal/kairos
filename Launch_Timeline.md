# Telos Launch Timeline — Go-to-Market (Jun 27–Aug 11)

Runs in parallel with [Software_Timeline.md](Software_Timeline.md), same 7 weeks, same Aug 11 end date. The split in strategy:

- **Jun 27–Aug 11 (this file):** build visibility, build relationships, build a warm pipeline of interested schools. Do **not** push for signatures yet.
- **After Aug 11 (separate section at the bottom):** schools are back in session and counselors are reachable/decision-ready — that's when signing actually happens.

The reasoning: school counseling offices are largely dark over summer. Pushing for a signed contract in July gets you a slow "let me get back to you in the fall" at best. The summer is for becoming the obvious choice by the time they're back — so the fall conversation is "yes, let's start" instead of "what is this."

---

## The three people to use, and what each one is actually for

| Person | What they're for | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Young Founders Lab camp mentor | Company growth strategy — positioning, GTM sequencing, how to not waste the summer | One structured call per week, every week of this timeline |
| The contact who might know interested schools | Warm introductions to actual counselors/admins — this is your sales pipeline source, not a generic networking contact | Reach out Week 1 to scope what schools/relationships they actually have; follow up as intros materialize |
| The software development advisor | Sanity-check technical decisions and de-risk the Software_Timeline in parallel | Async/as-needed, lower cadence than the growth mentor — pull them in specifically before Week 3 (security) and Week 4 (billing/infra), the two highest-stakes technical weeks |

**Acceptance criteria for this section:** by end of Week 1, all three contacts have been reached out to with a specific ask (not just "let's catch up") and a first meeting/call is scheduled with at least the growth mentor and the schools contact.

## Week 1: Jun 27–Jul 3 — Foundations + first outreach
- [ ] Register Telos as an LLC, get an EIN, open a business bank account, get a business credit/debit card — this gates the Stripe business-verification item in Software_Timeline Week 1, and is a hard requirement for school procurement, which generally won't contract with an unincorporated individual
  **Acceptance criteria:** the LLC is registered with the state, EIN is issued, and a business bank account + card are open in the company's name, not a personal account.
- [ ] File the LLC operating agreement, and if there's more than one founder, document ownership splits in writing now
  **Acceptance criteria:** a signed operating agreement exists; if multiple founders, equity/ownership percentages are documented before any revenue or outside money is involved.
- [ ] Set up basic bookkeeping (e.g. QuickBooks or a simple system) from day one, separating all business income/expenses from personal finances
  **Acceptance criteria:** every business expense and the first Stripe revenue, once live, flows through the business bank account and is recorded, not mixed with personal spending.
- [ ] Check whether a state/local business license or sales-tax registration is required to sell to schools in your state
  **Acceptance criteria:** a written determination exists on licensing/sales-tax obligations, with any required registration filed before the first real sale.
- [ ] Get a quote on general liability + tech/professional liability (E&O) insurance, given you're handling minors' data and signing contracts with institutions
  **Acceptance criteria:** at minimum a quote and a documented decision (buy now vs. defer to first signed school) exists — don't let this be silently skipped.
- [ ] Run a trademark/name conflict check on "Telos" before going wider publicly
  **Acceptance criteria:** a basic search (USPTO database + general web search) turns up no obvious conflicting active use of the name in education/software.
- [ ] Set up a business email domain (e.g. you@telos.com) and route it through the same domain as the product
  **Acceptance criteria:** outbound emails to mentors, schools, and press come from a telos.com address, not a personal Gmail.
- [ ] Call with Young Founders Lab mentor: present the product, the Aug 11 deadline, and ask specifically for help sequencing the next 6 weeks of go-to-market
  **Acceptance criteria:** leave the call with a written list of 3+ concrete tactics to execute this timeline, not just general encouragement.
- [ ] Reach out to the schools contact: ask specifically which schools/counselors they know, how warm the relationship is, and whether an intro now (for a fall conversation) or later makes more sense
  **Acceptance criteria:** a named list of 2+ specific schools or counselors exists, each tagged with relationship warmth (cold/warm/hot) and likely intro timing.
- [ ] Define the ideal customer profile (ICP): what kind of school/counseling office is the best first target (school size, public/private, counselor-to-student ratio, region)
  **Acceptance criteria:** a one-paragraph ICP exists that the schools contact and any future outreach can be filtered against.
- [ ] Set up a simple landing-page email capture ("counselor waitlist" / "get notified at launch") distinct from the student-facing product
  **Acceptance criteria:** a counselor visiting the marketing site can leave their email and school name without needing to sign up for the full product.
- [ ] Set up basic GTM metrics tracking (waitlist signups, follower growth per platform, link clicks from social bios) reviewed weekly alongside the mentor check-ins, so the rest of this timeline isn't six weeks of activity with no way to tell what's working
  **Acceptance criteria:** a simple tracker (spreadsheet is fine) exists with these numbers logged weekly starting this week.
- [ ] Update your personal LinkedIn profile (not just the company page) to clearly reflect founding Telos — headline, about section, pinned post
  **Acceptance criteria:** a school admin or journalist who looks up your name, not just the company, immediately understands who you are and what you're building.
- [ ] Reserve the Instagram handle now (username + bio link only, no posts, no follows yet) purely to prevent squatting; reserve LinkedIn company page and X account the same way
  **Acceptance criteria:** all 3 handles are claimed under the same name/logo, but Instagram stays dormant (no posts, no following) until Week 5–6 below.
  **Status (Jun 27):** Instagram done. X signup stuck on a disabled Continue button on the "set up your account" screen — retry tomorrow with a fresh browser/mobile app per troubleshooting notes; not an availability issue.
- [ ] LinkedIn + X can go active immediately (lower-stakes, founder/counselor audience, doesn't depend on the landing page being polished); active Instagram use — following people, posting, the things that actually drive bio-link traffic — waits until the landing page's mobile-first pass (Software_Timeline Week 3) and broader polish are further along, so the page is ready to receive that traffic well instead of looking unfinished when someone you just followed checks the bio link
  **Acceptance criteria:** a specific trigger date/week is set for "go active on Instagram" tied to landing-page readiness, not just a calendar guess.
- [ ] Decide the split between platforms: LinkedIn + X for counselors/ed-tech/founder audience, Instagram for students and parents (more visual, more casual)
  **Acceptance criteria:** a one-paragraph note exists on what content style goes where, so posts aren't just copy-pasted identically across all 3.

## Week 2: Jul 4–10 — Content engine + counselor-association research
- [ ] Identify the 3–5 school counselor associations/communities relevant to your region (state counselor associations, NACAC, college counseling Slack/Facebook groups)
  **Acceptance criteria:** a named list of associations exists, each with whatever's needed to post/engage there (membership, posting rules, upcoming events).
- [ ] Publish first piece of founder-led content (LinkedIn post or short blog post) about the problem Telos solves — written from genuine insight, not generic ed-tech marketing copy
  **Acceptance criteria:** one piece is published and shared in at least one relevant community from Week 2's research.
- [ ] Start a content cadence on LinkedIn + X: one post per week through launch (counselor/founder audience). Instagram stays dormant per Week 1's decision — it joins the cadence in Week 5–6 once the landing page is ready for the traffic it'll drive.
  **Acceptance criteria:** a content calendar exists mapping each remaining week to one LinkedIn/X post, with Instagram explicitly marked as starting later.
- [ ] Mentor check-in: review Week 1's outreach results, adjust ICP/messaging if the schools contact's leads suggest a different angle
  **Acceptance criteria:** any change in ICP or messaging is documented with the reasoning.

## Week 3: Jul 11–17 — Warm pipeline building
- [ ] Follow up with the schools contact on any intros that have materialized; have a first informal conversation (not a pitch) with at least one counselor to validate the problem
  **Acceptance criteria:** at least one real conversation with a counselor or school admin has happened, with notes on what they actually said about their current pain points.
- [ ] Build a simple pipeline tracker (spreadsheet is fine) of every school/counselor contact made so far: name, school, warmth, last contact, next step
  **Acceptance criteria:** the tracker exists and is updated after every contact from this point forward.
- [ ] Second LinkedIn/X post (Instagram still dormant); engage (don't just post) in the counselor communities identified in Week 2 — answer questions, comment thoughtfully, build visibility before ever pitching
  **Acceptance criteria:** at least 3 genuine engagements (comments/replies) in counselor communities, with zero direct pitches.
- [ ] Pull in the software development advisor to sanity-check the security/RLS work happening in Software_Timeline Week 3
  **Acceptance criteria:** a conversation or async review has happened and any concerns are logged back into Software_Timeline.

## Week 4: Jul 18–24 — Case study / proof material + press kit
- [ ] Reach back out to Luke Taylor (YFL managing director, gave earlier UX feedback) to show him the project's progress — wait until now rather than Week 1 so there's a visible delta (counselor dashboard live, hardening done) instead of showing the same state he already reacted to

  **Acceptance criteria:** a one-pager exists that a counselor could forward internally without needing you on the call.

- [ ] Assemble a real press kit: boilerplate company description, founder bio + photo, product screenshots, logo files, the testimonial/quote gathered above
  **Acceptance criteria:** a single shareable folder/page exists with all of the above, ready to attach to any journalist email without assembling it under deadline.

## Week 5: Jul 25–31 — Directory & visibility push

- [ ] Follow up with every outlet that hasn't responded within ~5 business days — one pitch is not a PR campaign
  **Acceptance criteria:** every "pitched" entry on the target list has at least one documented follow-up before launch week.
- [ ] Ask the Young Founders Lab mentor specifically whether they have press contacts or have been featured anywhere that could open a door
  **Acceptance criteria:** the mentor has been asked this directly, not assumed to volunteer it.

  **Acceptance criteria:** pipeline tracker shows growth against the target set in Week 4.
- [ ] Draft a standard school agreement/MSA template (pricing terms, term length, data handling, termination) — this pairs with the DPA already tracked in Software_Timeline Week 5, and needs to exist before any real signing conversation in the post-Aug-11 phase

- [ ] Activate Instagram: by now the landing page's mobile-first pass and pricing page (Software_Timeline Week 3 + Week 5) should be live, so the page is actually ready for the traffic following people will generate. - [ ] Fourth round of posts on LinkedIn/X + Instagram's first post(s); consider asking the growth mentor to share/amplify if appropriate

## Week 7: Aug 8–11 — Launch
- [ ] Execute the launch announcement across every channel prepared in Week 6 (LinkedIn, X, Instagram, waitlist email, directories, any press contacted)
  **Acceptance criteria:** announcement goes out on or immediately after Aug 11, consistently across all channels.
- [ ] Personally follow up 1:1 with every warm/hot pipeline contact with the live link, inviting them to look (not yet to sign — see post-launch section)
  **Acceptance criteria:** every warm/hot contact receives a personal message, not just the broadcast announcement.
- [ ] Send the live link to every friend/family contact from Week 6 and explicitly ask each one to share it with their own parents, school parent group chats, or community Facebook groups — this is the actual amplification step, not just the earlier heads-up
  **Acceptance criteria:** the launch link has been personally sent to every contact from Week 6's list with a direct ask to forward it onward, not just posted publicly and left to chance.
- [ ] This is also the week the real pilot counselor (Software_Timeline Week 7 item) uses the live product — capture their reaction as fresh launch-week proof
  **Acceptance criteria:** the pilot counselor's feedback/reaction is captured in writing for use in fall outreach.
- [ ] Publish a distinct "founding" announcement (not just a product post) stating Telos is a real, registered company — on LinkedIn at minimum, framed around the founder story, not just the feature list
  **Acceptance criteria:** the post explicitly states the company is founded/incorporated, not just that a product exists, so it reads as a company announcement to anyone seeing it cold.
- [ ] Set up a public company profile (Crunchbase, AngelList/Wellfound at minimum) listing the entity, founding date, and team
  **Acceptance criteria:** at least one such profile is live and accurately reflects the legal entity formed in Week 1.
- [ ] Add a real "About/Company" page on the website itself (founder bio, mission, contact) distinct from the product marketing pages
  **Acceptance criteria:** a visitor can find who's behind Telos and why it exists without needing to find you on social media.
- [ ] **Public launch — August 11, with Telos publicly and verifiably a real company by August 12**

## After August 11 — school signing (separate phase, starts once schools reopen)

This intentionally starts *after* the launch timeline above, once counseling offices are staffed and counselors are reachable and decision-ready (typically early-to-mid September for the back-to-school ramp). Trying to get signatures in July/early August mostly wastes effort on people who aren't in a position to decide yet.

- [ ] Re-engage every warm/hot pipeline contact from the summer with a direct ask: a real conversation about signing on, not just "checking the product out"
  **Acceptance criteria:** every contact from the tracker gets a direct, individual outreach once school resumes — not a mass email.
- [ ] Convert the schools-contact's strongest lead into the first real signed school, using the pilot counselor's launch-week feedback as proof
  **Acceptance criteria:** at least one school is in active contract/signing conversation within 2 weeks of school resuming.
- [ ] Use the first signed school as the next case study to widen outreach to the rest of the pipeline
  **Acceptance criteria:** a second wave of outreach references a real signed customer, not just the product itself.
