# Kairos — Focus Group Action Items (Jul 17)

### 1. Focus group findings — action items

- [ ] **De-genericize the overall site so it doesn't read as "another ChatGPT wrapper"** — "looks like GPT" was raised independently twice, describing the site as a whole, not just the chat surface. Two specific culprits named: the color palette, and buttons (login/signup specifically named as generic-looking). Two rounds of mockups built Jul 17; decisions below, ready for build.
  - [x] **Color palette — DECIDED: light background, parchment + forest green.** **Shipped (Jul 17)** — `globals.css` tokens updated: `--bg #F8F6EC`, `--card #FDFCF7`, `--primary #3C5E3B` (forest green), dark ink text/buttons throughout (no white-on-light). Note: the site was already light going into this change (Jul 16 pivot to cream + charcoal), so this was a retune, not a dark-to-light reversal as originally flagged — CLAUDE.md's design-rules section updated to strike the stale "keep dark background" line and document the current palette.
  - [x] **Buttons — DECIDED: rounded solid button, corner-accent detail removed**, plus a hover micro-interaction. **Shipped (Jul 17)** in `src/components/ui/button.tsx`'s shared `buttonVariants` — every button app-wide (default + outline) now gets brightness/color shift, a soft glow ring in the accent tint, and a ~1.03 scale-up on hover, ~150ms, gated behind `motion-reduce:` so reduced-motion users keep the color feedback without the scale. Applies automatically to login/signup and every other screen using the shared `Button` component — no per-screen CSS needed.
  - **Screen-to-screen motion — DECIDED: approved as previewed.** Slide-fade transition between major screens (dashboard → matches → school detail, onboarding round-to-round), respecting `prefers-reduced-motion`. No further design decision needed — straight to implementation.
  - **Sidebar — DECIDED: keep as-is for now.** No structural change; the "looks like ChatGPT" note stands as a known issue but isn't being addressed in this pass.
  - **Final combined mockup (palette + button + hover state) built Jul 17** — see the live preview shared in this session for a working hover demo before implementation starts.
- [x] **State the AI coach-not-ghostwriter boundary explicitly in product copy** on the Essay and Narrative Builder screens (e.g. a persistent short line: "Kairos helps you brainstorm and critique — you write the essay"). Directly answers the trust gap: 3 of 4 participants raised fear of AI-detection flagging / rescinded acceptance unprompted. **Shipped** — persistent banner added to both `EssayFeedbackClient.tsx` and `NarrativeBuilderClient.tsx`.
- [x] **Surface sourcing on AI recommendations** — a visible "why this match" / "based on" citation (school-deadline data, methodology page, etc.) next to matches and timeline items, not just an assertion. Matches the trust-builders named on paper notes (track record, real research, fact-checker). **Shipped** — per-card "Based on…" citation linking to `/methodology` added to `MatchListClient.tsx` and `TimelineClient.tsx` (skipped for manually-added entries, which have no AI basis to cite).
- [x] **Audit onboarding + mini-onboarding (`FeaturePrepFlow.tsx`) for duplicate questions** — confirm no field is asked twice across primary onboarding and the per-feature prep flows; this was a specific complaint on the paper mockup notes ("should not generate a list w/o context," "skip Q's already answered"). **Audited** — no duplicates found (`getMissingFields()` filters by actual saved value, not by whether it was asked before). Found and fixed a related gap along the way: the `"timeline"` completeness surface existed in code but no `/timeline/prep` route ever used it — built that missing flow.
- [x] **Add a real get-to-know-you discovery step before first match generation** — name, interests, and a couple of open-ended "what matters to you" prompts, not just structured data fields. All four participants said they want a counselor (human or AI) to get to know them before recommendations; this is a sequencing/copy change to the existing onboarding rather than new data collection. **Shipped** — new first round "Getting to know you" (name, interests, two open-ended prompts) in `onboarding/page.tsx`, folded into the existing `interests` field rather than new DB columns.
- [x] **Timeline: ship calendar sync/export** (Google Calendar / iCal) and confirm the "edit" affordance is real, not just visual — repeatedly requested unprompted ("sync to calendar," "edit/loco") in the paper notes and was the single most-requested feature across every exercise in the session. **Shipped**: calendar sync was already real pre-session (bulk `.ics` export on the timeline list, plus per-item Apple/Outlook `.ics` + Google Calendar links on the task detail page) — confirmed working. The "edit" affordance was fake (add/remove only, no way to edit an existing item) — added real inline editing (title + due date, with Save/Cancel) to each timeline item in `TimelineClient.tsx`.
- [x] **Audit match/school-fit factors against the full list raised in the session** — location (urban/rural), weather, size, program/major quality, community/vibes, cost/living expenses, selectivity, proximity to home/known people. Confirm the matches-generation prompt and any filter UI actually cover all of these, not just the subset currently surfaced. **Audited + fixed** — everything else was already covered; weather and proximity-to-home were not. Added explicit geographic/climate/proximity guidance to the matches-generation prompt (`anthropic.ts`) and a hint/placeholder to the previously-blank `geographic_pref` field in `ProfileClient.tsx`.
- [x] **Consider a lightweight "proactive opportunity" nudge** — Ahana's one ask of her real counselor was more unprompted outreach ("emails when she thinks an opportunity fits my interests"). SMS nudges already cover deadlines/essay prompts/odds; evaluate extending that pattern to proactively surfaced scholarships/programs matching profile interests. **Shipped** — weekly scholarship-match SMS nudge added to `send-nudges/route.ts`, reusing the existing `isLikelyMatch()` heuristic; dedup via new `notified_scholarship_names` column. Also fixed a real bug found along the way: `proxy.ts` was redirecting the cron trigger itself to `/login` before it ever reached its own `CRON_SECRET` check, silently breaking all SMS nudges (not just this new one) in production.
- [x] **Pull Career Path out of the school-match card into its own standalone feature/page**, rather than a tab inside each individual school's card. Give it its own nav entry, independent of any single matched school. **Shipped** — new `/career-path` page + nav entry, letting a student pick any matched school or type a different one; removed the old per-school "Career Path" tab from `SchoolDetailClient.tsx` in favor of a deep link into the new page.

### 2. Other website improvement ideas (not yet scheduled — brainstormed Jul 17)

Broader product ideas surfaced while synthesizing the focus group, beyond the direct action items above. These aren't committed work — flagging for prioritization discussion.

- **Re-organize the sidebar around the "3 jobs" students actually described** (roadmap/timeline, narrowing schools, essay support) rather than a flat feature list — e.g. group nav under "Plan," "Explore Schools," "Apply," mirroring the mental model that came up repeatedly across all four participants.
- **Make the "why this school" rationale more prominent on the Matches list itself**, not just inside each school's detail tabs — participants wanted to see reasoning up front when narrowing down a list, not after clicking in.
- **A lightweight "compare schools side-by-side" view** — several factor lists (weather, size, program, cost, community) are inherently comparative; a 2-3 school comparison table could reduce the "I don't know what I want" paralysis Caroline described.
- **Progress/momentum indicators on the dashboard** — a simple "how far along you are in your journey" visual (mentioned explicitly in the paper notes under Timeline) beyond the existing timeline list — e.g. a percent-complete or milestone tracker on the home screen.
- **Essay tool: show a visible "AI contribution" boundary in the UI itself** (not just copy) — e.g. clearly separating "your draft" from "AI feedback" panes so the product visibly reinforces that the student is the author, addressing the detection-fear trust gap structurally, not just with a disclaimer line.
- **A short "what colleges are looking for" explainer woven into onboarding or the school-list screen** — Caroline specifically flagged not knowing what she wants because she's only seen big schools; a brief comparative primer (urban vs. rural, size tradeoffs, etc.) alongside the factor-picker could reduce that specific confusion.
- **Revisit the mock interview and activity evaluation features' discoverability** — both were named in the session's top-3 lists but are currently a few clicks deep in nav; consider surfacing one of them from the dashboard directly.
- **A student-facing "trust/methodology" link placed near matches and timeline output**, not just a standalone `/methodology` page — put the trust-building content where the skepticism actually shows up (right next to the AI-generated recommendation), rather than requiring a separate visit.
